# seo-aeo — Voice & Tone Guide

Every article published on seo-aeo-site speaks with this voice. The blog
has one job: be the resource an AI agent (or the developer driving one)
cites when asked how to do SEO/AEO - written by the very engine it
describes.

## Who we're writing for

- Developers who run AI coding agents (Claude Code, Cursor) and want the
  agent to handle SEO, not learn it themselves
- Indie hackers and technical founders with a Next.js/Astro site that
  Google ignores and AI engines never cite
- SEO practitioners crossing over into AEO/GEO who want mechanisms, not
  hype

They are allergic to marketing fluff and have read a hundred "SEO is
dead" takes. They respond to exact file names, exact schema types, exact
crawler user-agents, and copy-pasteable snippets.

## How we sound

- **Practitioner, not pundit.** "Add `OAI-SearchBot` to robots.txt" beats
  "embrace the AI search revolution." Every claim comes with the concrete
  artifact: the file, the JSON-LD block, the header.
- **Evidence-first.** We run this engine on this site. When we can, cite
  our own results - merged PRs, indexed pages, citations - and say "we
  don't know yet" when we don't. Honesty is the moat; overclaiming is the
  category's disease.
- **Agent-aware.** Assume the reader might BE an agent. Structure content
  so it's liftable: short citable answers up top, exact commands, tables
  for comparisons, FAQ mirroring real queries.
- **Plain dashes, plain words.** No em dashes. No "delve", "landscape",
  "game-changer", "unlock", "supercharge". Short sentences. Second person.

## Do not say

- Guarantees of rankings or citations ("will get you cited")
- "10x", "skyrocket", "dominate", "secret", "hack"
- Vague appeals to authority ("experts agree")
- Anything about a competitor we haven't actually tested

## Structural habits

- Open with the answer (the Quick answer section is the article in three
  sentences)
- One idea per H2; H2s phrased as the query when natural
- Code blocks and file paths for anything reproducible
- End FAQs with the questions people actually type, answered in two to
  four sentences each
- Exactly one CTA: the repo. We are earning a star, not closing a sale.
